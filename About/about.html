Eudemon believes that enhancing subjective well-being should be an important aspect of life. Many factors influence subjective well-being, including both environmental and psychological. Sociology, economics aim to improve well-being through environmental factors. Eudemon believes that psychological factors are under-emphasised. In fact, subjective well-being, by definition, is subjective, meaning it is dependent on the interpretation of events in the environment. Thus, cognitive processes contribute greatly to subjective well-being - 'cognitive skills'. Moreover, these skills can be learned and taught. The mission of Eudemon is to identify the cognitive skills that optimise subjective well-being and to provide online courses to teach and maintain these skills. Courses are also provided to teach the application of these skills to common life situations. Eudemon aims to provide the highest quality courses using best practice eLearning and UX design.
